Writing

I have always loved writing, but I wasn’t good at it. Other interests took up my time, and I didn’t want to practice. That was my problem—I simply didn’t practice, until finally I did. And when I did, it felt like I solved all my problems. School, like for so many people, killed my love for reading and writing. It wasn’t enjoyable when you were told to do it. But writing on my own time? That’s infinitely better.

Music

This is a fun one, but also integral to how I think. Now I play guitar, though when I was young I didn’t want to learn an instrument. I’m glad I did, because there’s something about the pure enjoyment of just playing for fun. The crazy part? I actually get better when I don’t play. I’ll take a day or two off, lose track of things, and somehow I come back better. Go figure!

The amount of music I seem to absorb now boggles me. I’ve always listened to a lot of music, across all sorts of genres. I constantly find myself with a song humming in my head. My favourite genre? Honestly, I’m all over the place—there’s no single answer!

Hiking

Got to love it. Who doesn’t love a good hike? The more I write these blurbs, the more it feels like I’m trying to reinvent Instagram. The thing is, I hate social media—there’s something corrupt about it, a general vibe I just can’t stand anymore. But back to hiking: it clears the head. Having a bad week? A hike will make it better. There’s something about walking that just gets the old brain moving.
